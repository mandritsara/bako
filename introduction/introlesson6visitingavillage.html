Malagasy-Tsimihety with Bako: Lesson 6 - Visiting a Village

During your time in Mandritsara, you might have the opportunity to visit villages around the region. This could be a day trip on foot/motorbike, or a few days with the community team by helicopter.

It is helpful to equip yourself with some basic vocabulary, including a few adjectives that might come in handy.

Common Vocabulary

  • Ny ambanivohitra = The village
  • Any ambanivohitra = To the village
  • Ny fiara = Car
  • Ny rivotra = Air/wind
  • Ny sakafo = Food/meal
  • Ny tabataba = Noise
  • Ny lanitra = Sky/heaven
  • Ny kintana = Stars
  • Ny alina = Night

Listen to adjectives in conversation:

Common Conversation

PERSON 1: Tsara ve any ambanivohitra? (Was the village visit good?)

PERSON 2: Ia, tsara satria...

  • Tsy betsaka ny fiara (There weren’t many vehicles)
  • Madio ny rivotra (The air was clean)
  • Malalaka (It was spacious)
  • Vaovao ny sakafo amin’ny ankapobeny (The food was generally fresh)
  • Tsisy tabataba (There wasn’t much noise)
  • Tsara tarehy ny lanitra sy ny kintana amin’ny alina (The sky and stars at night were beautiful)

Appendix: Adjectives

To construct a sentence with adjectives, the rule is the same as for verbs: there is no “IS/ARE” in Malagasy sentences.

Listen to adjective sentence structure:

Ways to Ask a Question Using Adjectives

  1. Manahoana / Manakory + noun/pronoun? (Answer: Adjective)
    E.g. Manahoana ny rivotra? - Madio. (How is the air? - Clean.)
  2. Adjective + ve + noun/pronoun? (Answer: Yes/No + Adjective)
    E.g. Madio ve ny rivotra? - Eny, madio. (Is the air clean? - Yes, it is clean.)
  3. Inona no + adjective? (Answer: Noun/Pronoun)
    E.g. Inona no madio? - Ny rivotra. (What is clean? - The air.)

Listen to adjective questions:

Common Sentences

  • Madio ny rivotra = The air is clean
  • Vaovao ny sakafo = The food is fresh
  • Tsara tarehy ny kintana = The stars are beautiful
  • Malalaka any ambanivohitra = It is spacious in the countryside
  • Tsisy tabataba = There is not much noise
