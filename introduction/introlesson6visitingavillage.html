Malagasy–Tsimihety Lesson 6: Visiting a Village

(Appendix: Adjectives) • November 2022

During your time in Mandritsara, you might have the opportunity of visiting villages around the region.

This could be a day trip on foot/motorbike, or a few days with the community team by helicopter. It is helpful to equip yourself with some basic vocabulary, including a few adjectives that might come in handy.

ANY AMBANIVOHITRA (to the village)

Using the list of vocabulary on the sidebar, see if you can decipher the following text:

PERSON 1: TSARA VE ANY AMBANIVOHITRA?

PERSON 2: IA, TSARA SATRIA
– TSY BETSAKA NY FIARA
– MADIO NY RIVOTRA
– MALALAKA
– VAOVAO NY SAKAFO AMIN’NY ANKAPOBENY
– TSISY TABATABA
– TSARA TAREHY NY LANITRA SY NY KINTANA AMIN’NY ALINA.

In plain English, it would sound like this:

How was your visit to the village?
It was good, because there weren’t many vehicles, the air was clean, the land was vast, the food was generally fresh, there wasn’t much noise, and the sky and stars at night were beautiful.

Question words (lesson 1 revision)

Ve = interrogative particle for asking a question
Fa maninonaoff/maninonatsm = why

Vocabulary

Nouns:
Ny ambanivohitra = the village
Any ambanivohitra = to the village
Ny fiaraoff/Tomobilinatsm = car
Ny rivotra = air/wind
Ny sakafooff/hanignytsm = food/meal
Ny tabataba = noise
Ny lanitraoff/langitrytsm = sky/heaven
Ny kintanaoff/Lakintanatsm = stars
Ny alina = night
Enyoff/Iatsm = yes
Tsiaoff/Ehe = no

Conjunctions:
Satria = because
Sy = and

Adverbs:
Amin’ny ankapobeny = in general
Amin’ny alina = during the night

Adjectives:
Tsara = good/well
Betsaka = many
Madio = clean
Malalaka = spacious
Vaovao = fresh/new
Tsara tarehy = beautiful/pretty

Adjectives

To construct a sentence with adjectives, the rule is the same as for verbs: there is no “IS/ARE” in Malagasy sentences.

ADJECTIVE + NOUN/PRONOUN

1. MADIO ny rivotra – the air is clean
2. VAOVAO ny sakafo – the food is fresh
3. TSARA TAREHY ny kintana – the stars are beautiful

Conversely, to pose a question with an adjective, there are 3 ways of doing so. We will use the example of “the air is clean”:

  1. MANAHOANAoff/MANAKORYtsm + NOUN/PRONOUN? → Answer (ADJECTIVE)
    e.g. MANAHOANA/MANAKORY NY RIVOTRA? MADIO
  2. ADJECTIVE + VE + NOUN/PRONOUN? → Answer (YES/NO + ADJECTIVE)
    e.g. MADIO VE NY RIVOTRA? ENYoff/IAtsm, MADIO or TSIAoff/EHEtsm, TSY MADIO
  3. INONA NOoff/INOtsm + ADJECTIVE? → Answer (NOUN/PRONOUN)
    e.g. INONA NO/INO MADIO? NY RIVOTRA

Let’s try replacing the noun with a pronoun, with the example of TSARA TAREHY IZY (He/she is beautiful). To ask a question, the structure is the same:

IZA NOoff/ZOVYtsm + ADJECTIVE?
IZA NO/ZOVY TSARA TAREHY? IZY

Revision of Rule for TSY/TSISY

1. TSY + ADJECTIVE = Tsy betsaka
2. TSISY + NOUN = Tsisy tabataba
