Malagasy–Tsimihety Lesson 6: Visiting a Village

Greetings, asking permission, meeting the village head, and customary polite phrases.

(Appendices: Gift‑giving & Etiquette • Helpful Travel Phrases)

Download PDF

Arriving & Asking Permission

You (at the edge of the yard): Mandroso ve? — May I come in?
Host: Mandroso e! — Please come in!
You: Misaotra betsaka. — Thank you very much.

Politeness at the door

  • Slight bow; extend the right hand while passing people.
  • Say azafady when moving through a crowd or past seated elders.

Meeting the Village Head

You: Aiza ny filohan’ny fokontanyoff / Aiza ny lohan‑tanànatsm? — Where is the village head?
Guide/Host: Eto akaiky / Any amin’ny …(place)….
You (greeting): Tonga soa no asaina? — May we be welcomed? (formal greeting)
Head: Tonga soa e. Mandrosoa fa … — Welcome. Please come in …

Titles & roles

  • fokontany — village administrative unit
  • filoha — leader/chair
  • lohan‑tanànatsm — head of village

Use respectful tone and avoid hands in pockets when greeting elders.

Offering a Small Gift

You: Manana kely fanomezana ho anareo. — We have a small gift for you.
You: Raiso azafady. — Please receive it.
Host: Misaotra indrindra. Hotahian’Andriamanitra ianareo. — Thank you very much. May God bless you.

Custom

Offer/receive with two hands. Often people say a short blessing in return.

Common gifts: rice, sugar, tea, soap; for elders, a lamba (cloth).

Practical Questions

You: Aiza ny rano fisotro? — Where is the drinking water?
You: Afaka mampiasa ny trano fidiovana ve? — May I use the toilet?
You: Aiza no andehanana any …(place)…? — How do we get to …?

Useful nouns

  • rano — water
  • trano fidiovana — toilet
  • lakozia — kitchen

Saying Goodbye

You: Misaotra tamin’ny fandraisana. — Thank you for receiving us.
Head/Host: Mandeha soa / Soava dia. — Safe travels.
You: Misaotra betsaka. Hiverina izahay. — Thank you very much. We will come again.

Leave‑taking

You can also say Mandrampihaona — see you later / until we meet again.

Appendix A: Gift‑giving & Etiquette

  • Use two hands when giving/receiving; a slight knee bend shows respect.
  • Seat elders first; if offered a tsihy (mat), sit with feet tucked in.
  • If declining food, do so gently: Misaotra, avy vao nihinana izahay — Thanks, we just ate.

Appendix B: Helpful Travel Phrases

  • Avy aiza ianao? — Where are you from?
  • Izaho avy any …. — I am from …
  • Hitady … izahay. — We are looking for …
  • Mbola lavitra ve? — Is it still far?
