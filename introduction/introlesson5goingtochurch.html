Malagasy-Tsimihety with Bako: Lesson 5 - Going to Church

To say "going to church," you say MANDEHA MIVAVAKA or MANDEHA MIANGONA , which literally means "going to pray" or "going to meet together."

In this lesson, I will take you through a typical church service, specifically a Baptist church, so that you do not get lost on your first Sunday here.

Common Church Vocabulary

  • Baiboly = Bible
  • Fihirana = Songbook
  • Fiangonana = Church (building or congregation)
  • Fanompoana = Service
  • Mamaky = Read

Typical Church Service Structure

  1. FIDERANA - Worship (Singing, Praying, Bible Reading, Testimonies)

    2. Listen to worship phrases:

  2. FANATITRA - Offering
  3. ANJARA HO AN’NY ANKIZY - Children's Lesson
  4. VAKITENY - Bible Reading (Usually the sermon passage)
  5. TORITENY - Sermon

    6. Listen to sermon phrases:

  6. FILAZANA VAOVAO - Announcements (Welcoming new visitors)
  7. TSODRANO - Benediction
  8. MIFANDRAY TANANA - Shaking hands before leaving

Common Phrases in a Church Service

  • Mitsangana - Stand up (Let us stand: HITSANGANA ANTSIKA )
  • Mipetraka - Sit down (We can sit: MAHAZOA TOERANA )
  • Mihira - Sing (Let us sing: HIHIRA ANTSIKA )
  • Hamaky ny Tenin’Andriamanitra - Read God’s word together
  • Mivavaka - Pray (Let us pray: HIVAVAKA ANTSIKA )

Appendix: Ordinal Numbers

In Malagasy, ordinal numbering is used for Bible references and song numbers.

  • First = Voalohany
  • Second = Faharoa
  • Third = Fahatelo
  • Last = Farany
