Malagasy–Tsimihety Lesson 4: Going to the Market

Phrases for asking prices, bargaining politely, and buying by weight or number.

(Appendices: Numbers for Prices • Quick Market Phrases)

Download PDF

Asking the Price

Customer: Ohatrinona ny vidiny azafady? — How much is it, please?
Alt. (pointing): Ohatrinona ity / ireny?
Vendor: Arivo ariary — One thousand ariary.

Tip

Ariary (Ar) is Madagascar’s currency. Prices are often rounded.

Point with an open hand; avoid pointing with a single finger.

Bargaining Politely

Customer: Afaka mihena kely ve? — Could you reduce (the price) a little?
Customer: Lasa be loatra. Ataovy … (price) … azafady. — That’s too much. Please make it …
Vendor: Eny, afaka / Tsy afaka. — Yes, I can / Sorry, I can’t.

Politeness

Use azafady and a friendly tone. Smile. Vendors appreciate brief greetings.

If the price is fixed, you might hear tsy mihen-draha (doesn’t go down) or simply “tsy afaka”.

Quantities & Units

Customer: Omeo iray kilao tompoko. — Please give me one kilo.
Customer: Akaiky antsasany kilao azafady. — About a half kilo, please.
Vendor: Tonga dia / Andraso kely. — Right away / One moment.

Units you’ll hear

  • kilao — kilo
  • rojo — a bunch (greens)
  • kirana — groceries
  • fonosana — a packet

Tsimihety often shortens vowels in fast speech; numbers are otherwise the same.

Appendix A: Numbers for Prices

When forming compound numbers:

  • 11–19: use ambin'ny (e.g., valo ambin'ny folo = 14).
  • 21–199: use amby (e.g., roapolo amby dimy = 25).
  • ≥ 200: link hundreds with sy (e.g., roanjato sy folo = 210).

Appendix B: Quick Market Phrases

  • Tsara ve? — Is it good/fresh?
  • Asehoy kely azafady. — Please show me.
  • Apesa kely. — Add a little (top up).
  • Ataovy roa. — Make it two.

Etiquette

Greet first (e.g., Salama; then Inona no vaovao? — any news?).

If you’re not buying after asking, say Misaotra, hiverina aho — thanks, I’ll come back.
