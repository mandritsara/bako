Malagasy–Tsimihety Lesson 4: Going to the Market

(Numbers, money and groceries)

Now that you have gained some basic expressions and grammar, this lesson will help you get ready to take on the local market and (hopefully) do your first shop!

We will cover: common shop / stall dialogue, “Misy … ve?”, market units, money, numbers, and the Malagasy way of joining numbers (ambin’ny, amby, sy).

1. At the shops

Customer: Haody (you say it if you don’t see the shopkeeper)

Shopkeeper: Mandroso

Customer: Salama tompoko

Shopkeeper: Salama tompoko

Customer: Misy atody ve? (are there any eggs?)

Shopkeeper: Ia, misy. (yes, there are)

Customer: Ohatrino(na)? (how much are they?)

Shopkeeper: Fitonjato (700)

Customer: Amezotsm / Omeooff telo (3) azafady (give me 3 please)

“Misy … ve?”

ve is an interrogative particle for yes/no questions.

Add it to a statement to make it a question.

Think of it as replacing the rising intonation in English.

2. At the market

Vendor: Mandroso

Customer: Ohatrino vary? (vary = rice)

Vendor: 700 Ar

Customer: Amezo telo (3) kapoaka azafady.

Vendor: Azafady tsisy la monnaie / tsisy madinika. (madinika = change)

“Mandroso”

Used to invite the customer to come closer or to buy, even in an open market stall.

Ask the price

If the price isn’t written, do ask. Prices are usually in Ariary.

3. Units of measurement at the market

  1. individual units
  2. Pile = toko
  3. Kilo = kiloa
  4. Litre = litatra
  5. Cups/tins = kapoaka

3 eggs = atody telo

3 piles of tomatoes = tomaty / voatabia 3 toko

3 kilos of sugar = siramamy 3 kilao

3 litres of milk = ronono 3 litatra

3 cups of rice = vary 3 kapoaka

Money (Vola)

1 Ar = 1 Ariary

Ariary × 5 = Franc Malagasy

When talking about money, ariary is used with 10 Ar (ariary folo) and 100 Ar (ariary zato) only.

For other values, the word is dropped, e.g. 200 Ar = roanjato.

In the market, prices generally start from about 100 Ar.

Franc vs Ariary

Malagasy Franc was replaced by Ariary in 2005 at 5 FMG = 1 Ar.

Some vendors still quote in Francs, especially to visitors.

Always check and know your 5× table.

Using connectors in numbers

Rule 1: Malagasy numbers are read from right to left.

Rule 2: The “and” connector is replaced with:

AMBIN’NY – for numbers between 11 to 19

AMBY – for numbers between 21 to 199

SY – for numbers beyond 200

Exercise

Try saying these sentences in Malagasy.

Ohatriño ny…

  1. How much is the rice?
  2. How much are the big onions? (note: big = be. Adjectives come after the noun.)
  3. How much is the flour?
  4. How much is the oil?
  5. How much is the chicken?

Misy… ve?

  1. Is there any sugar?
  2. Is there any milk?
  3. Are there any eggs?
  4. No meat
  5. No bread

Amezotsm / Omeooff …

  1. Give me 2 eggs please.
  2. Give me 2 big onions please.
  3. Give me 2 kilos of salt please.
  4. Give me 2 litres of oil please.
  5. Give me 2 cups of rice please.
  1. 1 kilo of meat
  2. 4 bananas
  3. 3 piles of tomatoes
  4. 5 tins of rice
  5. How much are the bananas? 200 Ar, or 3 for 500 Ar.
