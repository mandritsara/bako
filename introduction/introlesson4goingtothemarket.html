Introduction – Lesson 4: Going to the Market (Appendix: Numbers, Money and Groceries)

Now that you have gained some basic expressions and grammar, this lesson will help you get ready to take on the local market and hopefully do your first shop!

Manao bazary / Mandeha bazary (“shopping for groceries” / “going to the market”)

  1. At the shops
  2. At the market

At the shop: basic exchange

Customer: Haody (you say it if you don’t see the shopkeeper)
Shopkeeper: Mandroso
Customer: Salama tompoko
Shopkeeper: Salama tompoko
Customer: Misy atody ve? (are there any eggs?)
Shopkeeper: Ia, misy. (yes, there are)
Customer: Ohatrino(na)? (how much are they?)
Shopkeeper: Fitonjato (700)
Customer: Amezotsm/Omeooff telo (3) azafady (give me 3 please)

Grammar tip: ve

Misy … ve? = “Is there … ?”

ve is an interrogative particle used to elicit a “yes”/“no” answer. Think of it as replacing the rising intonation used in English questions.

Mandroso at the market

Even though there is no physical shop, mandroso is used to invite the customer to approach and buy.

At the market

Vendor: Mandroso
Customer: Ohatrino vary? (vary = rice)
Vendor: 700 Ar
Customer: Amezo telo kapoaka azafady. (give me 3 cups, please)
Vendor: Azafady tsisy la monnaie / tsisy madinika. (madinika = small change)

Units of measurement at the market

  • Individual units
  • Pile = toko
  • Kilo = kilao
  • Litre = litatra
  • Cups/tins = kapoaka

“3 of …” patterns (same audio as above)

  • 3 eggs = atody telo
  • 3 piles of tomatoes = tomaty/voatabia telo toko
  • 3 kilos of sugar = siramamy telo kilao
  • 3 litres of milk = ronono telo litatra
  • 3 cups of rice = vary telo kapoaka

Money (Vola)

1 Ar = 1 ariary

Ariary × 5 = FRANC Malagasy

When talking about money, ariary is used with 10 Ar (ariary folo) and 100 Ar (ariary zato) only. For all other values, the word is dropped, e.g. 200 Ar = roanjato. In the market, prices generally start from about 100 Ar.

Money note

Malagasy Franc was replaced by Ariary in 2005 at a rate of 5 francs = 1 Ar. You’ll still hear prices quoted in Francs, especially to foreigners. Always check if it’s Francs or Ariary — and know your 5× table!

Vocabulary

  • Atody = eggs
  • Mandroso = come and buy
  • Telo kapoaka = 3 cups
  • Telo toko = 3 piles
  • Tsisy madinikaoff / lamonnaietsm = no change
  • Vary = rice
  • Ronono = milk
  • Tomaty / voatabia = tomatoes
  • Siramamy = sugar

APPENDIX 1 – Numbers / Counting

(Ny Isa = numbers; manisa = to count)

Rule 1: Malagasy numbers are read from right to left.

Rule 2: The English “and” connector is replaced with: ambin’ny (11–19), amby (21–199), and sy (≥ 200).
