Appendix: Numbers, Money and Groceries

Now that you have gained some basic expressions and grammar, this lesson will help you get ready to take on the local market and hopefully do your first shop!

Manao bazary / Mandeha bazary = “shopping for groceries” / “going to the market”.

This lesson builds on Lessons 1–3 so you can actually buy things, ask “Is there…?”, and understand prices.
At the shop: basic exchange audio

Typical exchange when you enter a small shop:

Customer: Haody (you say it if you don’t see the shopkeeper)

Shopkeeper: Mandroso

Customer: Salama tompoko

Shopkeeper: Salama tompoko

Customer: Misy atody ve? (are there any eggs?)

Shopkeeper: Ia, misy. (yes, there are)

Customer: Ohatrino(na)? (how much are they?)

Shopkeeper: Fitonjato (700)

Customer: Amezo tsm / Omeo off telo (3) azafady. (give me 3 please)

Useful: “Haody” is your “hello?” to a shop; “mandroso” is the standard welcome/invitation to come in and buy.
Grammar tip: ve audio

Misy … ve? = “Is there … ?”

ve is the yes/no question particle.

Put ve after the focus of the question:
Misy atody ve? = Are there eggs?
Azonao ve? = Can you?
“Mandroso” at the market audio

Even in an open-air market with no walls, sellers will still say mandroso to invite you to come closer and look/buy.

Think of it as “come on in / come buy”. You can reply with “Salama” or just start asking “Ohatrino … ?”
At the market audio

Vendor: Mandroso

Customer: Ohatrino vary? (vary = rice)

Vendor: 700 Ar

Customer: Amezo telo kapoaka azafady. (give me 3 cups, please)

Vendor: Azafady tsisy la monnaie / tsisy madinika. (no change)

madinika = small change.
tsisy madinika or tsisy la monnaie → they want exact money.
Units of measurement at the market audio

You won’t always see a scale. You buy by container or pile:

  • Kapoaka = cup/tin (for rice, beans)
  • Toko = small pile (for tomatoes, veg)
  • Kilao = kilo
  • Litatra = litre (oil, milk)

“3 of …” patterns:

  • atody telo
  • tomaty telo toko
  • siramamy telo kilao
  • ronono telo litatra
  • vary telo kapoaka
Learn these 4 first:
kapoaka, toko, kilao, litatra.
Then just put the number in front.
Money (Vola) audio

1 Ar = 1 ariary.

5 Francs = 1 Ariary. Some people still quote in Francs.

Market prices usually start around 100 Ar. Always check which unit they mean.

If the price sounds x5 too high, they may be speaking in Francs.
“Franc sa Ariary?” = “Franc or Ariary?”
Vocabulary audio
  • Atody = eggs
  • Mandroso = come/buy
  • Kapoaka = cup/tin
  • Toko = pile
  • Tsisy madinikaoff / tsisy la monnaietsm = no change
  • Vary = rice
  • Ronono = milk
  • Tomaty / voatabia = tomatoes
  • Siramamy = sugar

APPENDIX 1 – Numbers / Counting audio

Ny isa = numbers; manisa = to count.

Rule 1: Malagasy numbers are read from right to left.

Rule 2: “and” changes form: ambin’ny (11–19), amby (21–199), sy (≥ 200).

Thousands Hundreds Tens Units
1 000 – arivo zato folo iray / araiky
2 000 – roa arivo roanjato roapolo roa / aroa
3 000 – telo arivo telonjato telopolo telo
10 000 – iray alina efajato efapolo efatra
20 000 – roa alina dimanjato dimampolo dimy
100 000 – iray hetsy eninjato enimpolo enina

Connector examples:

  • 11 = iraika ambin’ny folo
  • 17 = fito ambin’ny folo
  • 21 = iraika amby roapolo
  • 85 = dimy amby valopolo
  • 113 = telo ambin’ny folo amby zato
  • 214 = efatra ambin’ny folo sy roanjato
  • 716 = enina ambin’ny folo sy fitonjato

Exercise

Try saying these sentences in Malagasy.

Ohatrino ny …
  1. How much is the rice?
  2. How much are the big onions? (big = be; adjectives come after the noun.)
  3. How much is the flour?
  4. How much is the oil?
  5. How much is the chicken?
Misy … ve?
  1. Is there any sugar?
  2. Is there any milk?
  3. Are there any eggs?
  4. No meat.
  5. No bread.
Amezotsm / Omeooff …
  1. Give me 2 eggs please.
  2. Give me 2 big onions please.
  3. Give me 2 kilos of salt please.
  4. Give me 2 litres of oil please.
  5. Give me 2 cups of rice please.
  6. 1 kilo of meat.
  7. 4 bananas.
  8. 3 piles of tomatoes.
  9. 5 tins of rice.
  10. How much are the bananas? 200 Ar, or 3 for 500 Ar.
