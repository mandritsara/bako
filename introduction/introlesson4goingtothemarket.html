Malagasy–Tsimihety with Bako: Introduction – Lesson 4

Going to the market (Manao bazary / Mandeha bazary)

Now that you have gained some basic expressions and grammar, this lesson will help you get ready to take on the local market and (hopefully) do your first shop!

Scope of this lesson

Basic market dialogue, units, money, and the number rules you wanted (ambin’ny, amby, sy).

1. At the shops / market

Manao bazary / Mandeha bazary = “shopping for groceries” / “going to the market”.

Customer: Haody

Shopkeeper: Mandroso

Customer: Salama tompoko

Shopkeeper: Salama tompoko

Customer: Misy atody ve? (Are there any eggs?)

Shopkeeper: Ia, misy. (Yes, there are.)

Customer: Ohatrino(na)? (How much are they?)

Shopkeeper: Fitonjato. (700 Ar.)

Then ask for the quantity:
Amezotsm/Omeooff telo azafady. = “Give me 3 please.”

▶︎ Audio: Dialogue – at the shop

“Misy … ve?”

ve = question particle.

Misy atody ve? = “Are there any eggs?”

2. Units of measurement at the market

These are the units the vendors will use:

  • Individual units
  • Pile = toko
  • Kilo = kilao
  • Litre = litatra
  • Cups / tins = kapoaka

Tip

Vendors will happily switch between cups, piles and kilos. Just repeat the unit you want: telo kapoaka, roa toko, iray kilao.

3. Money (Vola)

1 Ar = 1 ariary. Ariary × 5 = Malagasy Franc.

In practice, people say ariary for 10 Ar (ariary folo) and 100 Ar (ariary zato), but for other values they often drop “ariary” and just say the number, e.g. 200 Ar = roanjato.

Prices in the market often start from about 100 Ar. Vendors may still quote in “Francs”, so always check whether it’s Ariary or Francs — and remember your 5× table!

Customer: Ohatrino vary? (How much is the rice?)

Vendor: 700 Ar.

Customer: Amezo telo kapoaka azafady. (Give me 3 cups please.)

▶︎ Audio: Market phrases

Ariary vs Franc

Madagascar switched to Ariary in 2005 at 5 Francs = 1 Ariary. Older sellers may still quote in Francs.

Appendix 1 – Numbers / Counting

NY ISA = numbers; manisa = to count.

Rule 1: Malagasy numbers are read from right to left.
Rule 2: connectors:

  • ambin’ny – for 11–19
  • amby – for 21–199
  • sy – for numbers beyond 200
