Malagasy-Tsimihety with Bako: Lesson 4 - Going to the Market

Now that you have gained some basic expressions and grammar, this lesson will help you get ready to take on the local market and hopefully do your first shop!

Manao bazary / Mandeha bazary

(“shopping for groceries” / “Going to the market”)

Units of measurement at the market

  • Individual units
  • Pile = toko
  • Kilo = kilao
  • Litre = litatra
  • Cups/tins = kapoaka

Money (Vola)

1 Ar = 1 Ariary

Ariary x 5 = FRANC Malagasy

When talking about money, Ariary is used with 10 Ar (ariary folo) and 100 Ar (ariary zato) only. For other values, the word is dropped, e.g., 200 Ar = roanjato.

Common Market Conversations

Customer: Haody (you say it if you don’t see the shopkeeper)

Shopkeeper: Mandroso

Customer: Salama tompoko

Shopkeeper: Salama tompoko

Customer: Misy atody ve? (Are there any eggs?)

Shopkeeper: Ia, misy. (Yes, there are.)

Customer: Ohatrino(na)? (How much are they?)

Shopkeeper: Fitonjato (700)

Customer: Amezotsm/Omeooff telo (3) azafady (Give me 3 please.)

Vocabulary

  • Atody = eggs
  • Mandroso = come and buy
  • Vary = rice
  • Ronono = milk
  • Tomaty/Voatabia = tomatoes
  • Siramamy = sugar

Appendix 1: Numbers & Counting

NY ISA = NUMBERS

ISA => MANISA = TO COUNT

Rule 1: Malagasy numbers are read from RIGHT to LEFT

Rule 2: The use of the connector "and" is replaced with:

  • AMBIN’NY - for numbers between 11 to 19
  • AMBY - for numbers between 21-199
  • SY - for numbers beyond 200

Appendix 2: Common Market Items

Exercise: Try saying these sentences in Malagasy

Ohatrino ny…

  • How much is the rice?
  • How much are the big onions?
  • How much is the flour?
  • How much is the oil?
  • How much is the chicken?

Misy…..ve?

  • Is there any sugar?
  • Is there any milk?
  • Are there any eggs?
  • No meat
  • No bread

Amezotsm/Omeooff…

  • Give me 2 eggs please.
  • Give me 2 big onions please.
  • Give me 2 kilos of salt please.
  • Give me 2 litres of oil please.
  • Give me 2 cups of rice please.
