Malagasy-Tsimihety with Bako

Introduction - Lesson 1a: Greetings

Hello! Welcome to our first lesson. My name is Bako. I have been a language tutor in Mandritsara since 2008, teaching missionaries and visitors to the Hopitaly Vaovao Mahafaly project.

The dialect spoken in Mandritsara is called Tsimihety, but official Malagasy is widely spoken here as well.

Let’s begin by learning how to say hello.

Common Greetings

In Mandritsara, the most common way of greeting each other is by saying:

Salama Tompoko - "Hello, sir"

Example exchange:

  • Person 1: Salama Tompoko!
  • Person 2: Salama Tompoko!
  • Person 1: Manakory ianao? (How are you?)
  • Person 2: Tsara fa misaotra. (Fine, thank you.)
  • Person 1: Ino vaovao? (What’s new?)
  • Person 2: Tsisy vaovao. (Nothing’s new.)

Greeting at a Friend’s House

When arriving at a friend’s house, the guest initiates the greeting by saying out loud:

Haody! - Much like knocking on the door.

The host responds: Mandrosoa. Mipetrapetraha. (Come in. Have a seat.)

Vocabulary

  • Salama = hello
  • Tompoko = sir
  • Tsara = good
  • Misaotra = thank you
  • Vaovao = new
  • Tsisy = nothing
  • Haody = hello
  • Mandrosoa = come in
  • Mipetrapetraha = have a seat
  • Mazotoa = enjoy

Language Notes

There are 18 tribes spread across 22 regions in Madagascar.

Other greetings in Madagascar:

  • Manao ahoana (Central, e.g. Tana)
  • Salama (North / Central)
  • Akory (East / South)
  • Miarahaba (Central)

Grammar Tip

Tsy = no, Tsisy = nothing. "Tsy" precedes verbs/adjectives, whereas "Tsisy" is usually preceded by a noun.
