Malagasy–Tsimihety Lesson 1a: Greetings

First contact phrases: hello, how are you, and welcoming a guest.

Download PDF

Hello!

Person 1: SALAMA — hello (any time of day)
Person 2: SALAMA e! / Salama tompoko — friendly/polite reply

Politeness particles

tompoko adds respect; e adds warmth.

Use a light bow and extend the right hand when passing people.

How are you?

Person 1: Inona ny vaovaooff? / Vaovao manahogytsm?
Person 2: Tsara fa misaotra. — Fine, thank you.
Follow‑up: Ary ianao? — And you?

Replies you’ll hear

  • Tsara ihany — pretty good
  • Tsy dia tsara — not so well
  • Mandoa hafatra — passing greetings/messages

Welcoming a Guest

Use Mandroso when a guest arrives; at mealtimes it can also be an invitation to join.

Host: MANDROSO e! — Come in!
Guest: MISAOTRA. — Thank you.
Host (meal): MANDROSO — Please join us.
Guest (polite reply): Misaotra, mazotoa. — Thanks, enjoy.

Body language

  • Offer/receive with two hands.
  • In Mandritsara, a slight knee bend shows respect.
