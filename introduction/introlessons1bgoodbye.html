Malagasy-Tsimihety with Bako

Introduction - Lesson 1b: Saying Goodbye

Additional Info

"Indray" = "koa", and can be used interchangeably.

To specify a time when you will meet again, add "again" or "koa" at the end.

Common Ways to Say Goodbye

  • Veloma tompoko
  • Veloma e
  • Mazotoa

Meeting Again at a Specific Time

  • Veloma/Mazotoa + harivariva koa (see you in the afternoon)
  • Veloma/Mazotoa + rahampitsooff / amarainytsm koa (see you tomorrow)
  • Veloma/Mazotoa + Alarobia koa (see you on Wednesday)

See You Later

  • REHEFA AVY EO (in a short while - minutes to hours on the same day)

Goodbye Before Eating

Person 1: Mazotoa homanatsm / mihinanaoff

Person 2: Misaotra ianao koa

Appendix: Days, Weeks, and Months

Parts of the Day

  • Morning: Marainaoff / Maraindrainytsm
  • Noon: Atoandrooff / Matsagnatsm
  • Afternoon: Harivaoff / Harivarivatsm
  • Night: Alina

Days of the Week

  • Monday: Alatsinainyoff / Tinainytsm
  • Tuesday: Talata
  • Wednesday: Alarobiaoff / Robiatsm
  • Thursday: Alakamisyoff / Kamisytsm
  • Friday: Zoma
  • Saturday: Asabotsyoff / Sabotsytsm
  • Sunday: Alahadyoff / Lahady-Dimansytsm

Months of the Year

Both English and French influenced words exist in Malagasy. Tsimihety prefers the French versions.

  • January: Janoaryoff / Zanvietsm
  • February: Febroaryoff / Fevrietsm
  • March: Martsaoff / Marsatsm
  • April: Aprilyoff / Avrilytsm
  • May: May
  • June: Jona
  • July: Jolay
  • August: Aogositra
  • September: Septambra
  • October: Oktobra
  • November: Novambra
  • December: Desambra
