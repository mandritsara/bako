Come In / Join Me

You will have encountered the word MANDROSO in Lesson 1a, which is what you say to a guest when they arrive at your house.

If you are having a meal or a drink and someone walks past, you can also say mandroso as a polite way of inviting them to join you.

Appendix: Malagasy Hospitality

There is a Malagasy proverb, “Valala iray ifanapahana,” which literally means, “we can share a cricket between us.” This expresses the idea of joy in sharing food.

Another proverb, “Hani-masaka tsisy tompony,” means “the meal is ready for everyone.” Hospitality is a core part of Malagasy culture.
