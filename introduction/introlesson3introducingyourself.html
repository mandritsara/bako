Talking About Your Family

After this obligatory introduction, you can talk about your family, spouse, and children.

To say that you are married, you say: Manambady zaho.

To say that you are single, you say: Tsy manambady zaho.

To say that you have children, you say: Mananjanaka zaho.

Common Vocabulary

  • Anarana = Name
  • Avy = Come (from)
  • Any = There
  • Asa = Work
  • Faly = Happy
  • Manambady = Married
  • Zanaka = Child
  • Lehilahy = Boy/Man
  • Vehivavy = Girl/Woman

Appendix: Basic Grammar Rules

1. The subject of the sentence is at the end instead of the beginning.

2. "To be" or "to have" does not exist in Malagasy.

3. Nouns do not have singular/plural forms.

4. No grammatical gender differences like in French.

5. Malagasy uses possessive suffixes.
