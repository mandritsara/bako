Malagasy–Tsimihety Grammar Lesson 13D

Possessive Pronouns

Malagasy–Tsimihety with Bako

1. Possessive Pronouns

These are pronouns (replacing nouns) that indicate ownership. They vary only according to the person (the possessor).

Subject pronoun Official (off) Tsimihety (tsm) Meaning
Aho / zaho — "I" AHY NIHINAHY "mine"
Ianao / ana(o) — "you" (sg.) ANAO NIHINĀ(O) "yours"
Izy — "he / she" AZY NIHINAZY "his / hers"
Isika / antsika — "we" (incl.) ANTSIKA NIHINTSIKA "ours"
Izahay / zahay — "we" (excl.) ANAY NIHINAY "ours"
Ianareo / are(o) — "you" (pl.) ANAREO NIHINARE(O) "yours"
Izy ireo / zareo — "they" AZY IREO NIHINJARE(O) "theirs"
I Soa (proper name) AN'I SOA NIHIN'I SOA "Soa's"
Ny dokotera — "the doctor" AN'NY DOKOTERA NIHIN'NY DOKOTERA "the doctor's"
Questions:  An'iza …? (off)   Nihinjôvy …? (tsm)   "Whose is …?"

2. Say Whose It Is

Ask and answer the question as in the example, using the table in section 1 above.

Example:

AN'IZA ny mofo? (off)  →  ahy

NIHINJÔVY ny môfo? (tsm)  →  Nihinahy

(Whose bread is this? — It's mine.)   MOFO / zaho  (bread / I)

4. Reading Dialogue — Listen and Understand

— Bokinjôvy tôho? <boky>

(Whose book is this?)

— Nihinahy.

(It's mine.)

— Ary nihinjôvy reto sezy reto? <sezy>

(And whose are these chairs?)

— Sezin'ny ankizy. <ankizy>

(They belong to the children.)

— Ary latabatra zôvy tôho? <latabatra>

(And whose table is this?)

— Latabatry ny mpampianatray. <latabatra – mpampianatra>

(It's our teacher's table.)

— Penin'ny mpampianatrareo koa ve reto? <penina – mpampianatra>

(Are these also your teacher's pens?)

— Ehe, nihin'i Soa.

(No, they belong to Soa.)

— Ary nihinjôvy ny baolina? <baolina>

(And whose is the ball?)

— Baolintsika.

(It's ours.)

— Ary ny solomaso? <solomaso>

(And the glasses?)

— Nihinao!

(They're yours!)

— Nihinahy ve tôho solomaso tôho?

(Are these glasses mine?)

— Ia, nihinao!

(Yes, they're yours!)

Key words: Ary = and  |  Koa = also  |  Ia = yes  |  Ehe = no  |  Tôho = this  |  Reto = these  |  Ve = question particle
