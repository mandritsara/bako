Malagasy–Tsimihety Lesson Grammar Lesson 3: Intransitive Verbs

(Malagasy–Tsimihety with Bako — Grammar Lesson 3) • March 2024

Read the text, then look at the grammar rules that follow.

This lesson continues the idea from Grammar Lessons 1–2: Malagasy sentences put the predicate first. Here, we see it in natural narrative text using intransitive verbs, past tense (M → N), and negation.

Reading Text

Andro tsy fiasana nomoaligny satria tinainin’ny Paska, tsy niasa zaho.

Tsy nianatra ny gaomadiniky, mahazany nandeha nitsangatsangana zaho sy ny fianakaviako.

Nisoma, nihira, nisakafo zahay.

Nody zahay tamin’ny 4.

What this lesson shows

Natural narrative still keeps Verb → Subject.

Nisoma zahay.

Nihira zahay.

Nody zahay tamin’ny 4.

Grammar notes from the text

1. Predicate (verb) before subject

Malagasy sentences do not start with the subject. The subject comes at the end.

VERB – SUBJECT

Nitsangatsangana zaho sy ny fianakaviako.

Nisoma zahay.

2. M → N → H time change

If the verb starts with M-, it changes to N- for past and H- for future.

mihira → nihira → hihira

3. Negation with “tsy”

For negation, place TSY before the verb.

Tsy niasa zaho

Tsy nianatra ny gaomadiniky

4. Question patterns (Manino / Zovy / Ve)

We can ask about the action, the doer, or just confirm with “ve”.

Misoma ny gaomadiniky

Q: MANINO ny gaomadiniky? → A: misoma

Q: ZOVY misoma? → A: ny gaomadiniky

Q: Misoma VE ny gaomadiniky? → A: Ia, misoma / Ehe, tsy misoma

5. Personal subject pronouns (Tsm / Off)

zaho / aho

ianao

izy

antsika / isika

zahay / izahay

areo / ianareo

zareo / izy ireo

i Soa

Quick recap

1. Verb first

2. M → N → H for time

3. “Tsy” before the verb

4. Manino / Zovy / Ve for questions
