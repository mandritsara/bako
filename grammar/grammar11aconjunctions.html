Conjunctions

June 2025 — Malagasy–Tsimihety with Bako

Frequent Conjunctions in Malagasy

Conjunctions are words or groups of words used to connect two or more words or phrases.

Example:

Lafo ny hena. Lafo ny atody. (Meat is expensive. Eggs are expensive.)
→ Lafo ny hena SY ny atody. (Meat and eggs are expensive.)

Misakafo izy. Miborosy nify izy. (He is eating. He is brushing his teeth.)
→ Misakafo izy, AVY EO miborosy nify. He is eating, then brushing his teeth.

Vocabulary

Nouns

  • ny lafarinina – flour
  • ny atody – egg
  • ny mofo – bread
  • ny hena – meat
  • ny tsaramaso – beans
  • ny andro – day / weather
  • Alahady – Sunday
  • dokotera – doctor
  • Malagasy – Malagasy person / language
  • Anglisy – English (person / language)
  • ny vady – spouse / husband or wife

Adjectives

  • ratsy – bad
  • tsara – good
  • lafo – expensive
  • mora – cheap
  • lavitra – far
  • malaky – fast

Verbs

  • miasa – to work
  • mitsangatsangana – to go for a walk
  • mandeha malaky – to go fast
  • mandeha miasa – to go to work
  • manao bazary – to do shopping
  • tara – to be late
  • mianatra – to study
  • mihazakazaka – to run
  • marary – to be sick

Exercise 1

Make a sentence with one of these conjunctions: satria (because), rehefa (when), raha (if), and translate to English.

Example:
Ratsy ny làlana satria avy ny orana.
