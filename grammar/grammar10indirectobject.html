1. Review: Basic Sentence Patterns

Previously, we saw basic Malagasy sentence structure:

Predicate – Subject

Type Malagasy English
Adjective Tsara ny andro The weather is nice.
Ravoravo izy He / She is happy.
Verb Misasa izy He / She is washing.
Mitomany izy He / She is crying.
Noun Mpampianatra i Soa Soa is a teacher.

We can make sentences longer by adding details:

Tsara be ny andro → The weather is very nice
Misasa amin’ny savony izy → He / She is washing with soap
Mampianatra frantsay tsara izy → He / She teaches good French

2. What is an Indirect Object?

It answers the question:

  • Ho an’iza?off → For whom?
  • Ho anjôvy?tsm → For whom?

It is introduced by HO (“for”). It can come before or after the subject.

VERB – INDIRECT OBJECT – SUBJECT

Example:
Manao mofo ho an’ny ankizy izy.
(He makes bread for the children)

Q: Manao mofo ho an’iza / ho anjôvy izy?
A: Ho an’ny ankizy.

ADJECTIVE – SUBJECT – INDIRECT OBJECT

Example:
Mamy ny mofo ho an’ny ankizy.
(The bread for the children is sweet)

Q: Mamy ny mofo ho an’iza / ho anjôvy?
A: Ho an’ny ankizy.
