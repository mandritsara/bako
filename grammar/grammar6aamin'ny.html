Malagasy–Tsimihety Grammar Lesson 6a

Using “Amin’ny” for Instrumental Complements

September 2024 — Malagasy–Tsimihety with Bako

The Malagasy preposition amin’ny (or tamin’ny in the past tense) is very commonly used, and its meaning depends on the context of the sentence. In this lesson, we will focus on amin’ny and tamin’ny, which mean "with" when referring to an object or instrument.

To ask questions, use the following phrases:

Amin’ny ino?tsm

Amin’ny inona?off

For past tense:

Tamin’ny ino?tsm

Tamin’ny inona?off

1. Structure of the Sentence

The general structure for sentences using amin’ny is as follows:

Verb + amin’ny + object/instrument + subject

Note: In the past tense, the verb must be conjugated in the past, and amin’ny becomes tamin’ny.

2. Tenses

Example Sentence Translation Tense
Nisotro rano tamin’ny tavoahangy izy. He drank water from a bottle. Past
Misotro rano amin’ny tavoahangy izy. He drinks water from a bottle. Present
Hisotro rano amin’ny tavoahangy izy. He will drink water from a bottle. Future

Misotrooff / Migiakatsm = to drink
Ny rano = water
Ny tavoahangy = bottle

3. Questions

Example Sentence: Migiaka rano amin’ny tavoahangy izy. (She drinks water from a bottle.)

  1. Maninona izy? (What is she doing?)
    Answer: Migiaka rano (She drinks water.)
  2. Migiaka inona izy? (What is she drinking?)
    Answer: Rano (Water.)
  3. Zovy migiaka rano? (Who is drinking water?)
    Answer: Izy (She.)
  4. Migiaka dite moa izy? (Is she drinking tea?)
    Answer: Tsia, rano (No, water.)
  5. Migiaka rano amin’ny inona izy? (With what is she drinking water?)
    Answer: Amin’ny tavoahangy (With a bottle.)

Another Example: Miasa amin’ny solosaina izy. (She works with the computer.)

  1. Maninona izy? (What is she doing?)
    Answer: Miasa (She works.)
  2. Iza no miasa? (Who is working?)
    Answer: Izy (She.)
  3. Miasa amin’ny inona izy? (What is she working with?)
    Answer: Amin’ny solosaina (With a computer.)
  4. Miasa amin’ny solosaina ve izy? (Is she working with the computer?)
    Answer: Eny (Yes.)
