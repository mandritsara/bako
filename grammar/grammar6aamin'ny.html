Malagasy–Tsimihety Grammar Lesson 6a: Using “Amin’ny”

(Malagasy–Tsimihety with Bako — Grammar Lesson 6a) • November 2024

Learn how to use amin’ny (and tamin’ny) to express instruments and objects — with examples and guided exercises.

A. Introduction

The Malagasy preposition amin’ny (tamin’ny in the past tense) is very commonly used, and its meaning depends on the context of the sentence.

In this lesson, we focus on amin’ny / tamin’ny meaning “with” when referring to an object or instrument.

Amin’ny ino?tsm

Amin’ny inona?off

Tamin’ny ino?tsm

Tamin’ny inona?off

B. Structure of the Sentence

Verb – amin’ny – object / instrument – subject

In the past tense, the verb must be conjugated in the past, and amin’ny becomes tamin’ny.

Example Sentence Translation Tense
Nisotro rano tamin’ny tavoahangy izy He drank water from a bottle Past
Misotro rano amin’ny tavoahangy izy He drinks water from a bottle Present
Hisotro rano amin’ny tavoahangy izy He will drink water from a bottle Future

C. Asking Questions

Migiaka rano amin’ny tavoahangy izy.

She drinks water from a bottle.

  1. Maninona izy? — Migiaka rano
  2. Migiaka inona izy? — Rano
  3. Zovy migiaka rano? — Izy
  4. Migiaka dite moa izy? — Tsia, rano
  5. Migiaka rano amin’ny inona izy? — Amin’ny tavoahangy

Miasa amin’ny solosaina izy.

She works with a computer.

  1. Maninona izy? — Miasa
  2. Iza no miasa? — Izy
  3. Miasa amin’ny inona izy? — Amin’ny solosaina
  4. Miasa amin’ny solosaina ve izy? — Eny
