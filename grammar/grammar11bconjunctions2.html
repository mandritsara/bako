Malagasy–Tsimihety Grammar Lesson 11b

Common Conjunctions (continued)

June 2025 — Malagasy–Tsimihety with Bako

This lesson continues from Lesson 11a, which introduced 14 common Malagasy conjunctions. Here we practise conjunctions expressing cause–result, contrast, purpose, sequence, simultaneity, precaution, and exclusion.

Exercise 2 — Ataovy toy ny ohatra

(Follow the example)

Rewrite each sentence replacing satria (because) with noho izany (mahazany — so / therefore). The word order changes: the cause comes first, then noho izany, then the result.

Original: Tsy miasa izy satria mianatra Malagasy.

(He is not working because he is studying Malagasy.)

→ Mianatra Malagasy izy, noho izany tsy miasa.

(He is learning Malagasy, so he is not working.)

  1. Mitomany izy satria malahelo.
    (He is crying because he is sad.)
  2. Manao satroka ny ankizy satria mafana ny andro.
    (The children are wearing hats because it is hot.)
  3. Tsy miasa izy satria marary nify.
    (He is not working because he has toothache.)
  4. Mihinana vary aho satria Malagasy.
    (I eat rice because I am Malagasy.)
  5. Manasa tànana i Soa satria hisakafo.
    (Soa washes her hands because she is going to eat.)
Show answers
  1. Malahelo izy, noho izany mitomany.
    He is sad, so he is crying.
  2. Mafana ny andro, noho izany manao satroka ny ankizy.
    It is hot, so the children are wearing hats.
  3. Marary nify izy, noho izany tsy miasa.
    He has toothache, so he is not working.
  4. Malagasy aho, noho izany mihinana vary.
    I am Malagasy, so I eat rice.
  5. Hisakafo i Soa, noho izany manasa tànana izy.
    Soa is going to eat, so she washes her hands.

Exercise 3 — Adikao amin'ny teny Malagasy

(Translate into Malagasy)

Conjunctions needed:

even though / even if = na dia … aza

except = afa-tsy

fortunately / luckily = soa ihany fa

but = fa

  1. The road is bad even though it is not raining.
  2. He is still working even though he is tired.
  3. He washes his hands even if he is not going to eat.
  4. We work every day except Saturday and Sunday.
  5. We are learning Malagasy except Vao.
  6. There is a lot of work except Friday.
  7. There is a lot of work; fortunately, there are many workers.
  8. I want to go for a walk; luckily, the weather is good.
  9. He is Malagasy but he lives in France.
  10. We are sad but we don't cry.
  11. He is Malagasy but he does not eat rice every day.
Show answers
  1. Ratsy ny làlana na dia tsy avy ny orana.
  2. Mbola miasa izy na dia reraka.
  3. Manasa tànana izy na dia tsy hisakafo.
  4. Miasa isan'andro izahay afa-tsy Asabotsy sy Alahady.
  5. Mianatra Malagasy izahay afa-tsy i Vao.
  6. Betsaka ny asa afa-tsy Zoma.
  7. Betsaka ny asa, soa ihany fa betsaka ny mpiasa.
  8. Te-hitsangatsangana aho, soa ihany fa tsara ny andro.
  9. Malagasy izy fa mipetraka any Frantsa.
  10. Malahelo izahay fa tsy mitomany.
  11. Malagasy izy fa tsy mihinana vary isan'andro.

Exercise 4 — Adikao amin'ny teninao

(Translate into English)

Conjunctions to look out for:

mba h- = in order to / so that

avy eo = then / after that

sady = while / and also

samy = both

sao = in case / for fear that

  1. Mihazakazaka izy mba tsy ho tara.
  2. Nandeha bazary i Soa mba hividy atody sy lafarinina.
  3. Mianatra frantsay izy mba hiasa any Frantsa.
  4. Manasa tànana avy eo misakafo.
  5. Mihazakazaka izahay avy eo mandeha mianatra.
  6. Manasa lamba i Mama avy eo mahandro sakafo.
  7. Mihira izy sady mandihy.
  8. Geda ny manga sady mamy.
  9. Misakafo i Lita sady mamaky boky.
  10. Samy Malagasy i Soa sy ny vadiny.
  11. Samy Nosy i Madagasikara sy i Angletera.
  12. Samy nitomany izahay.
  13. Handeha taxi aho sao tara.
  14. Tsy nividy mananasy izy sao tsy mamy tsara.
  15. Manadio latabatra izy sao misy vitsika.
Show answers
  1. He runs so as not to be late.
  2. Soa went to the market to buy eggs and flour.
  3. She studies French in order to work in France.
  4. We wash our hands, then we eat.
  5. We run, then we go to school.
  6. Mum washes clothes, then she cooks.
  7. She sings while dancing.
  8. The mangoes are big and sweet.
  9. Lita eats while reading a book.
  10. Both Soa and her husband are Malagasy.
  11. Both Madagascar and England are islands.
  12. We all cried.
  13. I'll take a taxi in case I am late.
  14. He didn't buy pineapple for fear that it wasn't ripe.
  15. He cleans the table in case there are ants.
