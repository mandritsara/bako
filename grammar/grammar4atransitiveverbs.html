Malagasy–Tsimihety Lesson Grammar Lesson 4a: Transitive Verbs (I)

(Malagasy–Tsimihety with Bako — Grammar Lesson 4a) • October 2024

MANASA HISAKAFO — a short text to introduce Malagasy transitive verbs.

This lesson is the next step after intransitive verbs: now the verb needs an object. We will look at the text first, then list the verbs and nouns, then study the rule Transitive verb – Object – Subject, then finish with object vs subject pronouns and the 4 question forms.

Reading text

MANASA HISAKAFO

Niantso an’i Soa aho. Nanasa azy hisakafo aho rahampitso.

Niantsena aho, nividy akoho sy legioma.

Nividy lafarinina sy atody ary mananasy koa aho. Hanao mofomamy amin’ny mananasy aho.

Focus of 4a

Today: transitive verbs — the verb needs an object.

manasa hisakafo → invite (someone) to eat

We will link verb → object → subject → time.

Grammar rules — Transitive verbs

For transitive verbs, you need a direct object, which could be a noun, pronoun, person, object, or animal.

Transitive verb – Object – Subject

Niantso an’i Soa aho. (I called Soa.)

Nanasa azy aho. (I invited her.)

Nividy akoho sy legioma aho. (I bought chicken and vegetables.)

A Malagasy sentence does not start with the subject — the subject comes at the end.

You can also add the time word after the subject:

Nanasa azy hisakafo aho rahampitso.

Verb – Object – Subject – Time.

If the verb begins with M-, it changes to N- in the past and H- in the future.

manasa → nanasa → hanasa

mividy → nividy → hividy

Compare

Intransitive: Verb – Subject

Transitive: Verb – Object – Subject – (Time)

a) Personal pronouns (object and subject)

We need object forms because transitive verbs act on someone/something.

Object pronouns Subject pronouns
Ahy (me) Aho / Zaho (I)
Anao (you) Ianao / Anao (you)
Azy (him/her) Izy (he/she)
Antsika (us, inclusive) Isika / Antsika (we, inclusive)
Anay (us, exclusive) Izahay / Zahay (we, exclusive)
Anareo (you, plural) Ianareo / Areo (you, plural)
Azy ireo (them) Izy ireo / Zareo (they)
An’i Soa (Soa) I Soa (Soa)

Examples:

Hanasa azy aho. — I will invite her.

Niantso an’i Soa aho. — I called Soa.

Tip

If your verb is acting on a person, you nearly always need the object form: an’i … / azy / ahy / anao.

Subject still goes at the end.

b) Question forms for transitive sentences

You can ask four questions for each sentence:

  1. Maninona / Manino — What are … doing? (past: Naninona / Nanino, future: Haninona / Hanino)
  2. Iza no / Zôvy — Who … ?
  3. An’iza / Zôvy — Who is the action directed to?
  4. Inona / Ino — What … ?

Ve / Moa turns it into a yes/no question.

1. Niantso an’i Soa aho

Maninona ianao? – Niantso an’i Soa

Iza no niantso an’i Soa? – Zaho

Niantso an’i Soa ve ianao? – Eny

Niantso an’iza ianao? – I Soa

2. Nividy akoho sy legioma zaho

Manino ianao? – Nividy akoho sy legioma

Zôvy nividy akoho sy legioma? – Zaho

Nividy akoho sy legioma ve anao? – Ia

Nividy ino anao? – Akoho sy legioma
