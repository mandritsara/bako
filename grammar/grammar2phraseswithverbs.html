Malagasy–Tsimihety Lesson Grammar Lesson 2: Phrases with Verbs

(Malagasy-Tsimihety with Bako — Grammar Lesson 2) • January 2024

PREDICATE — SUBJECT, with the predicate as a VERB (intransitive)

The structure of a Malagasy sentence is “PREDICATE – SUBJECT.” In this lesson, the predicate is a VERB, more precisely an “Intransitive Verb”, which is a verb that does not require a complement.

In Malagasy, verbs always remain the same, while the subject changes. Since there is no gender or number, there is no need for agreement between the verb and the subject, unlike European languages in general.

However, verbs do undergo a bit of a change to help provide a sense of time – you may have noticed that most indicative verbs start with the letter “M.” We change “M” to “N” to denote the past and “H” for the future.

The subject can be a pronoun or a noun.

1. Basic verb sentences

Miasa izy — He/she works

Niasa izy — He/she worked

Hiasa izy — He/she will work

Now with the different personal pronouns (Tsimihety first, official Malagasy in brackets):

Miasa zaho (aho): I work

Miasa anao (ianao): You work

Miasa izy: He/she works

Miasa antsika (isika): We work

Miasa zahay (izahay): We work

Miasa areo (ianareo): You work

Miasa zareo (izy ireo): They work

Miasa I Soa: Soa works

Miasa ny vitsika: The ants work

To form questions to the sentences above:

Manino (Maninona) = What are we doing?

Zovy (iza no) = Who is?

Ino (inona no) = What is?

Ve = Is it / Do … ?

Verb time markers

Most indicative verbs start with M-

M → N for past

M → H for future

miasa → niasa → hiasa

same subject, verb carries the time

2. More examples + question forms

Example 3 (showing how to question each tense):

Miasa ny vitsika

– Manino ny vitsika ? – miasa

– Ino miasa ? – ny vitsika

– Miasa ve ny vitsika ? – Ia, miasa izy

Niasa izy

– Nanino ny vitsika ? – niasa

– Zovy niasa ? – izy

– Niasa ve izy ? – Ia, niasa izy

Hiasa i Soa

– Hanino i Soa ? – hiasa

– Zovy hiasa ? – i Soa

– Hiasa ve i Soa ? – Ia, hiasa

Negation with verbs

For negation, add “TSY” before the verb.

Miasa izy → Tsy miasa izy: He works / He does not work

Niasa zaho → Tsy niasa zaho: I worked / I did not work

Hiasa i Soa → Tsy hiasa i Soa: Soa will work / Soa will not work

Question words (review)

Manino / Maninona — why / what are we doing

Zovy / Iza no — who

Ino / Inona no — what

“ve” — yes/no

Apply to each tense: present (M), past (N), future (H).
