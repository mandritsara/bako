Malagasy–Tsimihety Grammar Lesson 6b

Using “Amin’ny” and “Tamin’ny” in Malagasy

December 2024 — Malagasy–Tsimihety with Bako

In the previous lesson, we learned about the preposition amin’ny or tamin’ny, which can mean “with” (and sometimes “in,” “on,” or “by,” depending on the context and sentence structure).

1. Basic Usage of Amin’ny

Structure:

amin’ny + noun

Example: amin’ny tavoahangy (in/with the bottle)

Verb + amin’ny + noun + subject

Example: Misotro rano amin’ny tavoahangy izy
(He drinks water from a bottle.)

2. Exercises

a. Answer the questions:

Sentence: Mahandro sakafo amin’ny saribao i Soa.
(Soa cooks food with charcoal.)

Questions:

Manino i Soa?
(Why does Soa cook?)

Answer: …

Iza no mahandro sakafo amin’ny saribao?
(Who cooks food with charcoal?)

Answer: …

Mahandro sakafo amin’ny inona i Soa?
(With what does Soa cook food?)

Answer: …

Mahandro inona amin’ny saribao i Soa?
(What does Soa cook with charcoal?)

Answer: …

Mahandro sakafo amin’ny saribao ve i Soa?
(Does Soa cook food with charcoal?)

Answer: …

b. Ask questions:

Sentence: Nihinana tamin’ny tànana izy.
(He ate with his hands.)

Questions:

……………… ? – Nihinana tamin’ny tànana
(What did he do?)

……………… ? – Izy
(Who ate with hands?)

……………… ? – Tamin’ny tànana
(With what did he eat?)

……………… ? – Ie, nihinana tamin’ny tànana izy.
(Did he eat with his hands?)
