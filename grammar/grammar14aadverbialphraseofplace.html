Malagasy–Tsimihety Grammar Lesson 14A

Adverbial Phrase of Place

Malagasy–Tsimihety with Bako

Introduction

The adverbial phrase of place is a group of words indicating the place where someone is, or where an action takes place. It answers the question AIZA / TAIZA (in the past) — meaning "where?"

In Malagasy, the place name is always preceded by an important word called a "locative". This word specifies the location — whether the place is near or far, whether it is visible or not, and whether the speaker is at that place or not. In the past tense, a "T" is added to the front of the locative.

Locative  +  Place name

Example 1: Eto Madagasikara. (Here in Madagascar — the speaker is in Madagascar.)

Example 2: Any Madagasikara. (There in Madagascar — the speaker is away from Madagascar.)

The difference: in example 1, the locative is eto (here), so the speaker is in Madagascar. In example 2, the locative is any (there / over there), so the speaker is far from Madagascar.

Example Dialogue

Aiza mipetraka ny Ray aman-dReny? (Where do the parents sit?)

Eto. (Here.)

Ary ny mpampianatra? (And the teacher?)

Eo. (There — nearby.)

Ary aiza mipetraka ny mpianatra? (And where do the students sit?)

Etsy sy erȳ. (Here and there.)

Vocabulary

Aiza — where  |  Eto — here  |  Eo / Etsy / erȳ — there

Ny Ray aman-dReny — the parents  |  Ny mpampianatra — the teacher  |  Ny mpianatra — the students

Sy / ary — and  |  Mipetraka — to sit

Types of Place

Places are classified into 3 groups, each with a different rule.

Group 1 — Proper place names

Proper place names such as Madagasikara, Frantsa, Angletera, Mandritsara. The rule is:

Locative  +  Place name

Examples (when the speaker is in Mandritsara / Madagascar):

Any Frantsa — Any Angletera (over there in France — over there in England)

Eto Madagasikara — Eto Mandritsara (here in Madagascar — here in Mandritsara)

Group 2 — Common place nouns

Common place nouns such as trano (house), lālana (street), fiangonana (church), zaridaina (garden). The rule is:

Locative  +  AN-  +  place noun
Locative  +  AM-  +  place noun (used when the place noun starts with p or b)

Following Malagasy word-combination rules, the first letter of the place noun changes as follows:

f → p    l → d    s → ts    z → j    r → dr    h → k    v → b

Examples:

TRANO = house    LĀLANA = street    FIANGONANA = church    ZARIDAINA = garden

Eto an-trano (off) / aketo an-trano (tsm) — here at home

Tany an-dàlana (off) / takany an-dàlana (tsm) — (it was) in the street [past]

Atsy am-piangonana (off) / arôho am-piangonana (tsm) — over there at church

Tao an-jaridaina (off) / takao an-jaridaina (tsm) — (it was) over there in the garden [past]

Group 3 — Specific places or foreign-origin names

Specific places or words of foreign origin such as Banky (bank), Hôpitaly (hospital), Bazary (market), Fiangonana Loteriana (Lutheran church). The rule is:

Locative  +  AMIN'NY  +  place noun

Examples:

BANKY = bank    HÔPITALY = hospital    BAZARY = market    FIANGONANA LOTERIANA = Lutheran church

Eto (aketo) amin'ny banky — here at the bank

Tany (takany) amin'ny Hôpitaly — (it was) at the hospital [past]

Ao (akao) amin'ny bazary — over there at the market

Tany (tarôho) amin'ny fiangonana Loteriana — (it was) over there at the Lutheran church [past]
