Malagasy–Tsimihety Grammar Lesson 13A

Possessive Adjectives

Malagasy–Tsimihety with Bako

1. A Special Group of Words

In Malagasy, there is a group of words (nouns and verbs) that does not follow the general grammatical rules. These are words that:

  • Have 3 or more syllables
  • End in -KA, -TRA, or -NA

For all ordinary Malagasy words, the stress falls on the penultimate syllable (second to last). But for this special group, the stress falls on the antepenultimate syllable (third to last). The position of the stress is very important for both pronunciation and comprehension.

Ma – so – a – ndro  →  masoandro (sun)

Sa – tro – KA  →  satroKA (hat)

Mpi – a – na – TRA  →  mpianaTRA (student)

Fi – a – na – ka – vi – a – NA  →  fianakaviaNA (family)

2. Possessive Adjectives

The possessive expresses the relationship between people, or indicates to whom something belongs. Possession is marked by a personal pronoun suffix added to the noun.

Malagasy nouns have no gender and no number, so possessives are invariable — they do not agree with the noun.

Noun (the thing possessed) + suffix (the possessor)

Example: Ny maso (eye / eyes) + zaho(tsm) (I)  →  Ny masoKO (my eye / my eyes)

3. Rules for Adding Possessive Suffixes

The suffix to add depends on how the noun ends:

  • If the noun does not end in -KA, -TRA, or -NA: add the suffixes directly: ko, nao, ny, ntsika, nay, nareo, n'izy ireo, n'i Soa, n'ny ankizy
  • If the noun ends in -NA: drop the -NA ending, then add the same suffixes.
  • If the noun ends in -TRA or -KA: drop the final -a (or the full -ka / -tra ending), then add the suffixes.
