Malagasy–Tsimihety Grammar Lesson 12

Indirect Personal Pronouns (AMIN')

May 2025 — Malagasy–Tsimihety with Bako

1. Introduction

In Malagasy grammar, instead of using prepositions like to, with, or from before a noun, Malagasy changes the form of the pronoun itself. Some verbs require an indirect complement — and for these, Malagasy uses a special set of indirect personal pronouns built around AMIN', rather than an English-style preposition.

English Malagasy
I talked to Lita. Niresaka tamin'i Lita aho.
He borrows a bike from me. Mindrana bisikileta amiko izy.
He works with us. Miaraka miasa aminay izy.

2. Sentence Patterns

These verbs slot into two main sentence structures:

Intransitive verb — indirect pronoun — subject

Example:

Mivavaka amin'Andriamanitra izy.

(He prays to God.)

Transitive verb — object — indirect pronoun — subject

Example:

Manazava lesona amin'ny mpianatra izy.

(He explains a lesson to the students.)

3. Indirect Personal Pronouns

When a sentence is in the past tense, the verb changes to past form (M to N), and the indirect pronoun gains a T at the front.

He works with us  →  Miaraka miasa aminay izy.

He worked with us  →  Niaraka niasa taminay izy.

5. Questions

  • Amin'iza? / Aminjôvy?  →  To whom? With whom? From whom?
  • In the past: Tamin'iza? / Taminjôvy?

Verb — … — amin'iza / aminjôvy — subject?

Question Answer
Mitovy amin'iza i Lita? (Who does Lita look like?) Mitovy amin'ny mamany izy. (She looks like her mother.)
Ary ny zandriny, mitovy amin'iza? (And the younger sibling, who are they like?) Mitovy amin'ny dadany. (Like their father.)

Note: Amin'iza / aminjôvy is used for people. For animals, objects, or other things, use amin'ny inona / amin'ny ino.

6. Reading Text: Mindrana bisikileta (Borrowing a bicycle)

Listen, read, and try to understand the dialogue.

— Hindrana bisikileta amin'i Lita ve ianao?

(Are you going to borrow a bicycle from Lita?)

— Een.

(Yes.)

— Efa niresaka taminy ve ianao?

(Have you already spoken to him/her?)

— Aan, mbola tsy niresaka izahay. Fa ahoana?

(No, we haven't spoken yet. Why?)

— Nilaza tamiko izy fa handeha any ambanivohitra.

(He/she told me that he/she is going to the countryside.)

— Dia handeha bisikileta ve izy?

(So is he/she going by bicycle?)

— Een, handeha bisikileta izy.

(Yes, he/she is going by bicycle.)
