Malagasy–Tsimihety Grammar Lesson 5: Adverbs and Common Auxiliary Verbs

(Malagasy–Tsimihety with Bako — Grammar Lesson 5) • September 2024

Learn commonly used adverbs and auxiliary verbs in Malagasy/Tsimihety—with examples, dialogue and exercises to practise.

A. Dialogue

Common expressions using adverbs and auxiliaries.

Q: Manakôry i Soa? — How are you, Soa?

A: Tsy tsaratsara zaho. — I’m not very well.

Q: Efa nizaha dokotera ve anao? — Have you already seen a doctor?

A: Tsabeky. — Not yet.

Q: Mila mandeha dokotera anao. — You need to go to the doctor.

A: Ia, angamba, fa tsy te-handeha dokotera zaho. — Yes, maybe, but I don’t want to go.

D. Exercises

1. Use the adverbs in brackets

Mafana ny rano (be – efa – tsara)

Kely ny zaza (loatra – efa tsy – mbola)

Niasa i Soa (mafy – mbola tsy – loatra)

Handeha izy (mbola – haingana – efa)

Homana vary zareo (tsara – loatra – be – efa – mbola – mbola tsy – efa tsy)

2. Practise the auxiliaries

Mandeha bazary izy — He/she is going to the market

Manao mofo izy — He/she is making bread

3. Listen and Repeat

To politely ask for a favour, use AFAKA + VE + AZAFADY.
To ask or give permission, prefer MAHAZO / MANJARY.
