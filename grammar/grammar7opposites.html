Malagasy–Tsimihety Grammar Lesson 7

Opposites in Malagasy

December 2024 — Malagasy–Tsimihety with Bako

Dialogue: Opposites in Conversation

Sarotra ve ny malagasy?
Is Malagasy difficult?

A-a, tsy sarotra, mora.
No, it’s not difficult; it’s easy.

Ary ny frantsay, sarotra ve?
And French, is it difficult?

Eny, sarotra.
Yes, it’s difficult.

Tianao ve ny frantsay?
Do you like French?

Eny, sarotra fa tiako.
Yes, it’s hard, but I like it.

Mianatra malagasy ve ianao?
Are you learning Malagasy?

A-a, tsy mianatra malagasy aho satria Malagasy.
No, I’m not learning Malagasy because I am Malagasy.

2. Grammar

In Malagasy, opposites can be formed in two ways:

  • Using a specific word (adjective, verb, etc.) with its opposite.
  • Adding the negation Tsy before the word.

Examples:

Sarotra ny malagasy.
Malagasy is difficult.

Tsy sarotra ny malagasy. → Malagasy is not difficult.
Tsy sarotra = Mora → Not difficult = Easy.

Mianatra malagasy aho.
I’m studying Malagasy.

Tsy mianatra malagasy aho. → I’m not studying Malagasy.
