# ============================================================
# CORE GRAMMAR RULES
# ============================================================

SENTENCE STRUCTURE:
- Malagasy sentences do NOT start with the subject. The subject comes LAST.
- Order: PREDICATE (verb/adjective/noun) — SUBJECT
- Examples:
  Miasa izy. (He works.) — NOT "Izy miasa."
  Tsara ny andro. (The weather is fine.)
  Mpampianatra i Soa. (Soa is a teacher.)

TENSE (verb prefix system):
- M- = present:  Miasa (works/is working)
- N- = past:     Niasa (worked)
- H- = future:   Hiasa (will work)
- Rule: replace M with N for past, M with H for future.
- Examples: Mihira / Nihira / Hihira (sing/sang/will sing)

NEGATION:
- Place TSY before the verb/adjective.
- Tsy miasa izy. (He doesn't work.)
- Tsy tsara ny andro. (The weather is not good.)

YES / NO:
- Yes: Eny (off) / Ia (tsm)
- No: Tsia / A-a (off) / Ehe (tsm)

QUESTION PARTICLE VE / MOA:
- Add "ve" or "moa" to turn a statement into a yes/no question.
- Miasa ve izy? (Does he work?)
- Answer: Ia / Eny (yes) or Ehe / Tsia (no)

"NY" = the definite article (the, a, le, la, les):
- ny andro = the weather/day
- ny trano = the house

|-----------------|----------------|-----------------|
| I / me          | aho / ahy      | zaho            |

[GRAM-16a] The Passive Voice (I)
URL: (not yet published — draft available)
Topics: Passive voice is natural and frequent in Malagasy — emphasises the OBJECT not the action.
- Active: VERB – object – subject (emphasis on action/subject)
- Passive: PASSIVE VERB – subject/agent suffix – object (emphasis on object)
- Example pair:
  Active: Manasa ovy izy. (He washes potatoes — emphasis: washing)
  Passive: Sasany ny ovy. (The potatoes are washed — emphasis: potatoes)
- Common active→passive verb pairs:
  Manasa → Sasana (wash), Manadio → Diovina (clean), Mahandro → Andrahoina (cook)
  Mitetika → Tetehana (chop), Mividy → Vidiana (buy), Mamboly → (plant)
  Manao → Atao (do/make), Mamaofy/Magnofy → (peel), Mandroso → Aroso (serve)
  Mamaky → Vakina (read/peel), Mila → Ilaina (need), Manisy → Asiana (add)

# PREPOSITIONS OF PLACE / LOCATIVES
# ============================================================

LOCATIVE PRONOUNS (distance + visibility):
- ETO / AKETO (tsm): here — close and visible
- ATO / AKATO (tsm): here — close, not visible
- EO / AKEO (tsm): there — close, visible
- AO / AKAO (tsm): there — close, not visible
- ETSY/ERY / ARÔHO (tsm): a bit far, visible
- ANY / AKANY (tsm): far, not visible
- ENY / AKENY (tsm): in the air/space
- Past tense: add T- → eto → TETO, any → TANY

PREPOSITIONS WITH PEOPLE (amin'ny → personal):
- Eto amiko (here with me), Any aminao (there with you)
- Aminy (with him/her), Amintsika/Aminay (with us), Aminareo (with you pl.), Aminjareo (with them)

# ============================================================
# CULTURAL NOTES FOR MISSIONARIES
# ============================================================

- Mandritsara is in the Sofia region, northern Madagascar. Tsimihety is the local dialect.
- Greeting: SALAMA TOMPOKO is the standard greeting in Mandritsara.
- HAODY = equivalent of knocking when arriving at someone's home.
- TOMPOKO = polite address (sir/ma'am) — use liberally.
- AZAFADY = please/excuse me/sorry — very versatile word.
- Body language: use both hands when giving/receiving; slightly bow when walking past; remove hat entering houses, passing a funeral.
- Family: extended family structure, cousins = siblings, uncles/aunts = parents.
- Church: Baptist church common in Mandritsara; services at 9am, Sunday school at 8am.
- Market: prices in ARIARY (Ar); no change is common (tsisy monnaie/madinika).
- Rice: eaten 3 times a day; central to culture and agriculture.
- Zebu: symbol of wealth, central to ceremonies, farming, and daily life.
- 18 tribes across 22 regions — each with dialect variations.
- Two seasons: hot/wet (Oct-Mar) and cool/dry.
- Traditional medicine (herbal) widely used alongside modern medicine.
- Zagnahary/Andriamanitra = God (Creator); ancestors play intermediary role in traditional belief.
