Malagasy–Tsimihety Theme Lesson 10: Telling the Time

(Malagasy–Tsimihety with Bako — Theme Lesson 10) • June 2025

The Time – Ny ora sy ny fotoana

Red = Tsimihety  |  Blue = Official Malagasy

The concept of time

The concept of time or the hour is one of the causes of frustration or misunderstanding for foreigners who work with Malagasy people or live in Madagascar. There's even an expression: "Fotoan-gasy" (Malagasy time), and also "Manao fotoan-gasy ny Malagasy" (Malagasy people are not on time).

It's 50% true – 50% false, because we think with two different systems. Foreigners have the hour and use a watch, but Malagasy people have time and use other systems like shadows, life events, animal calls, etc. That doesn't mean we don't use watches!

Examples:

  • The doctor tells the patient to return at 2 p.m. — it's more effective to say: "Come back after lunch." In that case, the patient might arrive even earlier. Waiting is not a big issue for Malagasy people.
  • When a Malagasy person gives someone an appointment, they say: "You can come after 4 because I won't be there before." → Not a precise time like "4:30".
  • "How long was your journey?" → "We left when the first rooster crowed and arrived at lunchtime." → Where is the number showing the trip's length in that answer?
  • "How many days were you coughing?" → "Since Tuesday last week."

1. Malagasy Time and the Hour

We can conclude, based on how Malagasy people — most of whom are farmers — know and speak about time (outside of clocks), that time is deeply tied to their environment and daily life. Malagasy use:

ALOKAoff / KANDINDAGNAtsm = shadow → Shadow position helps determine whether it's nearly midday or already afternoon.
MASOANDROoff / MASÔVAtsm = sun → The position and movement of the sun.
FEON-BIBY = animal sounds
Daily activities (e.g., pounding rice, cows leaving or returning)

Time-of-Day Words

To express the time, we use the numbers from 1 to 12, and we either rely on the context or add a time-of-day word. These time-of-day words help indicate whether we mean morning, noon, evening, etc.

Time Range Official Malagasy Tsimihety
~1 am – 10 amMarainaMaraindraigny
~11 am – 1 pmAtoandroMatsagna
~1 pm – 3 pmTolakandroFôlakandro
~4 pm – sunsetHarivaHarivariva
After sunset – midnightAlinaAligny

Numbers 1–12

1 iray • 2 roa • 3 telo • 4 efatra • 5 dimy • 6 enina
7 fito • 8 valo • 9 sivy • 10 folo
11 iraika ambin'ny folo • 12 roa ambin'ny folo

Examples

  • Amin'ny 1 atoandro and amin'ny 1 maraina = at 1 in the afternoon / at 1 in the morning
  • Amin'ny 12 atoandro = mitataovovonana = noon
  • Amin'ny 12 alina = misasakalina = midnight
  • Amin'ny 3 maraina and amin'ny 3 folakandro = 3 am and 3 pm
  • Amin'ny 6 maraina and amin'ny 6 hariva = 6 am and 6 pm

Asking & Answering: What time is it?

❓ Question:
Amin'ny firy izao azafady ?off
Amin'ny firy andro ?tsm
= What time is it please?
✅ Answer: Use the hour number + time-of-day word.
e.g. Amin'ny 3 sy sasany hariva = It's half past 3 in the afternoon.
e.g. Amin'ny 8 latsaka fahefany maraina = It's quarter to 8 in the morning.

4. Expressing Duration

Question:
Adiny firy … ?off
Firy lera … ?tsm
= How many hours … ?

✈ Example:

Adiny firyoff / Firy leratsm Frantsa – Madagasikara mandeha fiaramanidina ?
= How many hours is the flight from France to Madagascar?

→ Adiny 12off / 12 leratsm → 12 hours
