Malagasy–Tsimihety Theme Lesson 2: The House

(Malagasy–Tsimihety with Bako — Theme Lesson 2) • March 2024

Ny Tranooff / Ny Tragnotsm

Red = Tsimihety  |  Blue = Official Malagasy

1. Introduction

Traditional Malagasy houses are built with wood or raw earth. The roof is made of tafo bozaka (thatch) or tafo tailaoff / tafo tanimangatsm (tile / terracotta), typically consisting of 1 or 2 rooms: one room with a kitchen corner or 2 rooms, one of which is a lakozia (kitchen) and the other a tranobeoff / tragnobetsm (large room). Later on, houses became more modernised. Houses from different regions of Madagascar have their own characteristics. All doors of traditional Malagasy houses should always face west so that the house can benefit from the sunlight to sunset.

Note: Malagasy people often use the cardinal points for orientation:

North = avaratra  |  South = atsimo  |  East = atsinanana  |  West = andrefana

In Malagasy, tanana or vohitra means town or village. The size of a town is defined by the number of roofs or kitchens.

Key words from the introduction

Bozaka = thatch

Tafo tailaoff / tafo tanimangatsm = tile / terracotta

Lakozia = kitchen

Tranobeoff / tragnobetsm = large room

Avaratra = North  |  Atsimo = South

Atsinanana = East  |  Andrefana = West

Tanana / vohitra = town or village
