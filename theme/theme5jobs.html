2. Grammar Notes — Possessive Adjectives

Possessive adjectives in Malagasy are added as suffixes to nouns. For example:

Asako — My job

Asanao — Your job

Here are the possessive suffixes:

Subject Pronoun Suffix Example
Aho / Zaho-koAsako (my job)
Ianao-naoAsanao (your job)
Izy-nyAsany (his/her job)
Izahay-nayAsanay
Isika-ntsikaAsantsika
Ianareo-reoAsareo
Izy ireo-nyAsany

Sample dialogue

— Inona no asanao?

— Tantsaha aho

— Mpamboly sy mpiompy zany ianao?

— Ia, mamboly vary sy miompy akoho aho

— Ary ianao, inona no asanao?

— Sofera no asako

Tantsaha = farmer

Zany = so

Ary ianao = and you

Practice Exercise: Use the possessive suffixes with "asa" (job) to indicate different people's jobs.

3. Job Names Derived from Verbs

Many job names in Malagasy are derived from verbs. This is generally done by changing the initial letter M in the verb to Mp (with some exceptions).

M . . . . (verb) → Mp . . . . (job)

Mianatra (to study) → Mpianatra (student)

Exercise: Try to find the verb form for each job by answering:

MANINONA ny …?off / MANINO ny …?tsm

Manino ny mpianatra?

— Mianatra izy

(What does the student do? — He/she studies)
