Malagasy–Tsimihety Theme Lesson 6: Clothing and Colours

(Malagasy–Tsimihety with Bako — Thematic Lesson 6) • December 2024

Ny fitafiana sy ny loko

Red = Tsimihety  |  Blue = Official Malagasy

1. Clothing of the Malagasy

Clothing in Madagascar varies depending on the region of origin. Most people wear lamba (traditional cloth) by either mitafy (draping) or misikina (wrapping it around themselves).

There are clothes worn daily, some reserved for celebrations, and others for funerals or mourning. During mourning, people often wear dark or white clothing, with red being rarely used.

As Madagascar is a hot country, there aren't specific clothes for cold weather. Instead, people wear more layers to keep warm.

Women:

Generally have long hair, sometimes braided, and often wear dresses. In many regions, they also wear lambahoany (a type of traditional cloth).

Men:

Tend to wear hats more frequently than women.

In some regions:

People remove their shoes before entering a house.

According to Malagasy culture, hats are removed when entering a house, singing the national anthem, or encountering a funeral procession.

2. Types of Clothing (Karazana fitafiana)

In Malagasy, the verb manao (to wear) is used with clothing.

Manao zipo izy. → She is wearing a skirt.

3. Colours (Ny loko)

Dark-coloured clothes are typically worn during mourning, while bright-coloured ones are worn for celebrations.

Questions about colours (Ny fanontaniana):

1. Inona ny lokon'ny … ? → What colour is the …?

E.g. Inona ny lokon'ny zipo? → What colour is the skirt?

2. Inona ny loko tianao? → What is your favourite colour?

Note: Colour duplication

Some colour names can be duplicated to signify a more vivid or purer shade:

Fotsy → fotsifotsy

Mena → menamena

Mavo → mavomavo

Mainty → maintimainty

Maitso → maitsomaitso

E.g. zipo fotsy = zipo fotsifotsy (pure white skirt)
