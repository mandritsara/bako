Malagasy–Tsimihety Theme Lesson 3: Weather and Climate

(Malagasy–Tsimihety with Bako — Theme Lesson 3) • March 2024

Ny andro sy Ny toetr'andro

Red = Tsimihety  |  Blue = Official Malagasy

1. How do you ask about the weather?

How is the weather? = Manahoana ny andro?off / Manakôry ny andro?tsm

Don't forget the past and future tense rules:

Nanakory ny andro? (past)

Hanakory ny andro? (future)

Different types of weather conditions:

Rainy

Manorana ny andro

it's raining

Sunny

Mafana ny androoff

mahamay ny androtsm

it's hot

Windy

Mandrivotra ny andro

it's windy

Chilly

Mangatsiaka ny androoff

Manintsy ny androtsm

it's cold

When it's generally bad or good:

Ratsy ny andro = the weather is bad (no sun, with clouds)

Tsara ny andro = it's nice (with sun)

Descriptions

Mando = humid  |  Maina = dry

Mando ny rivotra = the wind is humid

Avy ny orana / latsaka ny orana = it's raining

Manga ny lanitra = the sky is blue

Manjavona ny andro = the weather is foggy

Mandrahona ny andro = it's cloudy

3. Madagascar's Two Main Climates

Madagascar is in the southern hemisphere, so the characteristics of the seasons are different from those in the northern hemisphere.

MAFANA sy MANDO

Hot and humid

October to March

MANINTSY sy MAINA

Cool and dry

April to September

The four Malagasy seasons:

Summer

Fahavaratraoff
asaratsm

Autumn

Winter

Lohataona

4. Notes & Exercises

  1. Using the words above, describe the weather in your area in December and July.

    Manakory ny andro any aminareo amin'ny volana Desambra sy volana Jolay?

  2. For Malagasy people in general, when we listen to the weather forecast, "good weather" means sunny and without rain. However, during the planting season, we need rain, and farmers do not agree with the concept of "bad weather" (with rain).
  3. The word "NY ANDRO" (which means day) is also used to describe the weather or climate.
  4. Madagascar is in the southern hemisphere, so the characteristics of the seasons are different from those in the northern hemisphere.
