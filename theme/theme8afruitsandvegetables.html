Madagascar: A Tropical Island

Madagascar is a large tropical island where more than 80% of Malagasy households work in agriculture. For some, it's their main source of income; for others, it's a secondary activity. The primary crop is rice.

Each region of Madagascar is known for specific crops, depending on its climate (hot, cold, humid, etc.) and soil type (volcanic, sandy, clay, etc.). Even in the southern regions, which face significant rainfall challenges, fruits and legumes (dry grain vegetables) are abundant.

The central, northern, and eastern regions are rich in fruits, while vegetables are plentiful in the highlands. Wild fruits are also part of the landscape.

Madagascar's lychee is well-recognised in some European countries and neighbouring islands.

Fruits and vegetables are seasonal. The only fruit available year-round is bananas.

The Malagasy consume fruits fresh and vegetables either raw or cooked, often served with rice.
